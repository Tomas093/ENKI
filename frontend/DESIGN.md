# Design System

## Core Aesthetic: Neo-Brutalist Pop (Industrial Print)

ENKI uses a tactile, unapologetic, and highly structured design language. It draws inspiration from Swiss industrial print, arcade game cabinets, and modern creator platforms (like Gumroad). The aesthetic is built on harsh contrast, physical-feeling interactions, and a strict, constrained color palette.

### Why constrained colors?
The richness in this design system does not come from a rainbow of colors, gradients, or soft shadows. It comes from **structure and motion**. By strictly limiting the palette to Background, Ink (Black), and one singular Accent (Yellow), the interface feels like a printed poster that has come alive. When you introduce too many colors to this style, it stops looking like an intentional, premium design and starts looking like a chaotic children's site. 

The visual interest is generated by:
1. Massive typographic contrast (huge black titles vs tiny tracking-spaced metadata).
2. Physical depth (solid offset shadows that compress when clicked).
3. Structural dividers (thick black borders separating every logical block).

## Palette

- **Background (Canvas)**: `#F4F4F0` (Matte Paper). We never use pure white `#FFFFFF` for the background.
- **Ink (Foreground)**: `#050505` (Carbon Black). Used for all text, borders, and shadows.
- **Primary Accent**: `#FFE234` (Neon Yellow). Used exclusively for the most critical actions, stakes (Prize Pool), and active states. If everything is yellow, nothing is yellow.
- **Surface**: `#FFFFFF` (Pure White). Used for cards, inputs, and components that need to pop off the matte paper canvas.
- **Functional Red**: `#E61919`. Used for error states and destructive actions.
- **Functional Green**: `#4AF626` (Terminal Green). Used sparingly for "Live" indicators and success states.

## Typography

- **Headings**: `var(--font-nunito)`. Must be used with extreme weight (`font-black`) and tight tracking (`tracking-tight` or `tracking-tighter`).
- **Body & Data**: `var(--font-dm-sans)`. 
- **Micro-labels**: Small, uppercase, heavily tracked. Example: `text-[10px] uppercase tracking-[0.1em] font-black`.

## Components & Patterns

### 1. The Brutalist Card
All containers, cards, and input fields share this DNA:
- **Borders**: Always `border-2 border-black`. No exceptions.
- **Corners**: Sharp or extremely subtle (`rounded-none` or `rounded-sm`). Never pill-shaped.
- **Shadows**: Static, solid offset shadows. Example: `shadow-[4px_4px_0px_#000]`. No soft blur (`blur-0`).

### 2. Arcade Buttons
Buttons must feel like physical plastic switches on an arcade cabinet.
- **Resting state**: `bg-[#FFE234] border-2 border-black shadow-[4px_4px_0px_#000] transition-all`.
- **Hover/Active state**: The button must physically depress. The shadow disappears and the button translates down and right to fill the space where the shadow was. `active:translate-x-1 active:translate-y-1 active:shadow-none`.

### 3. Textures
- **Scanlines**: The background features a subtle CSS repeating linear gradient to give it a CRT/printed texture, keeping the large matte paper areas from feeling dead.

## Motion (Microinteractions)

Motion is mechanical and abrupt, not smooth or easing. 
- Elements snap into place.
- Loading states use brutalist block-bouncing rather than smooth spinners.
- State transitions (like copying a code) immediately snap the background color to green and snap back.

## Anti-Patterns (What to avoid)
- ❌ **Glassmorphism / Backdrop Blur**: Removed entirely. It belongs in iOS, not here.
- ❌ **Soft Drop Shadows**: Never use standard `shadow-md` or `shadow-lg`. Shadows are solid black blocks.
- ❌ **Gradients**: No color-to-color gradients on buttons or text. 
- ❌ **Rounded Pills**: `rounded-full` is banned for buttons and containers (except for literal circular avatars or live dots).
